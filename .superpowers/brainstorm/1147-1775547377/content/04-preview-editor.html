Timeline Strip Interactions

How Users Edit the Long Screenshot
Trim Head/Tail Drag the orange handles at the top/bottom of the timeline strip to trim the beginning or end. The main viewport updates live to show the new boundary. Like trimming a video clip.
Cut Middle Click a segment in the timeline to select it (highlighted). Press Delete to cut it. Cut segments are shown struck-through with reduced opacity — they can be restored with Ctrl+Z. Multiple segments can be selected (Shift+click for range, Ctrl+click for multi) and cut at once.
Chrome Toggle The "Chrome: On/Off" toolbar button toggles whether fixed top/bottom/left/right regions are included in the output. Useful when you only want the scrolling content.
Viewport Navigation Scroll wheel to zoom. Click-drag to pan. "Fit" button fits entire image in view. "1:1" shows actual pixels. Clicking a segment in the timeline scrolls the viewport to that section.
Undo/Redo Ctrl+Z / Ctrl+Y for undo/redo. All trim and cut operations are reversible. No destructive edits until the user clicks Save.

Output Actions

What Happens When You're Done Editing
💾
Save
Composites final image (chrome + included segments).
Saves as PNG to configured folder.
Auto-names: ScrollShot_YYYYMMDD_HHmmss.png
📋
Copy
Composites final image and places on clipboard.
Ready to paste into any app.
Toast confirms success.
🗑️
Discard
Throws away the capture.
Confirmation prompt if edits were made.
Returns to idle state.
